Roll #12 — Sydney

Fuji 400 / Olympus OM-1 / ISO 400 / December 2025

Summer light around Circular Quay felt almost too bright for film, but that was the point. I let the roll keep its haze and softness instead of correcting everything.

This spread is mostly harbour air, ferry noise, and the little pause before sunset when the water starts looking silver.

Archival note: meter for the water, hold the highlights, trust the blue.

weekend notes

Roll #11 — Weekend

Kodak Gold 200 / Canon Autoboy / ISO 200 / November 2025

Nothing dramatic here. Just the neighbourhood holding still long enough for me to notice it. Warm grass, late sun, a narrow lane that felt cinematic for no real reason.

This roll reminded me that the ordinary is usually enough when the light is honest.

Archival note: keep the frame loose, leave space for air and shadow.

Roll #10 — Melbourne Dusk

Portra 400 / Pentax K1000 / ISO 400 / October 2025

Melbourne always gives me frames that feel half-document, half memory. I was chasing reflections and tram-window glare, trying to keep the city loose instead of polished.

The colour on this roll came out softer than expected, almost like the pages already aged before I opened the scans.

Archival note: Portra loves overcast evenings more than I do.
