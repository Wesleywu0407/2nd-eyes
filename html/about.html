ABOUT

Film photography is my way of slowing down.

A second look at ordinary life.

Why I shoot film

I started with film because I wanted to take fewer photos, not more. Digital felt too easy, too quick to fill a memory card and forget what you shot. With film, each frame costs something. You think before you press the shutter.

I like the waiting. The gap between shooting a roll and getting it back from the lab. Sometimes weeks pass, and by then you have almost forgotten what you captured. Opening fresh scans feels like finding old letters you wrote to yourself.

This site started as a way to keep track of my work: what film I used, where I was, what I noticed. It is becoming something closer to a diary. Not every shot is perfect, but that is the point.

Right now I am based in Australia, mostly shooting around the city and coast. It is all about noticing.

The best camera is the one that makes you want to go outside and look for something worth remembering.

What I shoot with

Nothing fancy. Just reliable tools that feel good to use.

Cameras

  • Canon AE-1 Program (35mm)
  • Pentax K1000 (backup)
  • Mamiya 645 (medium format)
  • Olympus Mju II (point and shoot)

Film

  • Kodak Gold 200 (everyday)
  • Kodak Portra 400 (people)
  • Ilford HP5 Plus (black and white)
  • Fuji Pro 400H (when I can find it)
∞

Process

  • Shoot until the roll is done
  • Lab develop and scan
  • Light editing in Lightroom
  • Print the favorites

What "2nd eyes" means

It is about looking twice. We walk past the same things every day and stop seeing them. Film photography makes me pay attention: the way light hits a building at 4pm, or how someone stands while waiting for the bus.

A second look at ordinary life. That is all this is.

What's here

  • Notes on what worked and what did not
  • Thoughts on different film stocks
  • A record of learning as I go
